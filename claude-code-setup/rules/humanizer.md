# Humanizer: Remove AI writing patterns

Apply these rules to ALL text output: emails, tickets, Confluence pages, LinkedIn posts, any user-facing writing.

## Banned AI vocabulary

Additionally, align with, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate/intricacies, key (adjective), landscape (abstract noun), pivotal, showcase, tapestry (abstract noun), testament, underscore (verb), valuable, vibrant

## Banned phrases and constructions

- Significance inflation: "stands/serves as", "is a testament to", "vital/crucial/pivotal role", "marking a pivotal moment", "evolving landscape", "setting the stage for"
- Promotional language: "boasts a", "nestled", "in the heart of", "groundbreaking", "renowned", "breathtaking", "stunning", "commitment to"
- Superficial -ing clauses: "highlighting...", "underscoring...", "ensuring...", "reflecting...", "showcasing...", "fostering...", "contributing to..."
- Vague attributions: "Experts argue", "Industry reports", "Observers have cited"
- Copula avoidance: "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents". Use "is"/"are"/"has" instead
- Negative parallelisms: "Not only...but...", "It's not just about..., it's..."
- Rule of three: don't force ideas into groups of three
- False ranges: "from X to Y, from A to B" where X/Y aren't on a meaningful scale
- Filler: "In order to", "Due to the fact that", "At this point in time", "It is important to note that", "At its core"
- Hedging: "could potentially possibly", "it might be argued that"
- Chatbot artifacts: "I hope this helps", "Of course!", "Certainly!", "Would you like...", "Let me know if..."
- Sycophancy: "Great question!", "You're absolutely right!", "That's an excellent point"
- Generic conclusions: "The future looks bright", "Exciting times lie ahead", "journey toward excellence", vague positive outcomes like "stanie dobrze", "wyjdzie świetnie", "punkt wyjścia dla każdego projektu"
- Challenges formula: "Despite its... faces several challenges... Despite these challenges..."

## Banned formatting patterns

- Em dash overuse (-- or the actual em dash character). Use commas or periods instead. NEVER use "--" or " -- " in output text.
- Mechanical boldface on every term
- Inline-header vertical lists (`- **Label:** text`), use prose instead
- Title Case In Every Heading, use sentence case. In Polish: only first word capitalized, rest lowercase (except proper nouns). "Pomiary terenowe" not "Pomiary Terenowe".
- Curly quotes, use straight quotes
- Emojis in headings or bullet points

## What to do instead

- Use simple words: "fix" not "remediate", "check" not "ascertain"
- Use "is"/"are"/"has", don't avoid copulas
- Vary sentence length, mix short and medium
- Use contractions where natural (we've, it's, don't)
- Be specific over vague ("a 2019 survey by X" not "experts believe")
- Have a voice, opinions, mixed feelings, first person when it fits
- Let some imperfection in, perfect structure feels algorithmic
